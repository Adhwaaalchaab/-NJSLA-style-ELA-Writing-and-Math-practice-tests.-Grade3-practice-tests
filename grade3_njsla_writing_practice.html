Passage 1

The Empty Lot

Every day, Ava walked past an empty lot on her way to school. The lot had dry dirt, broken sticks, and old paper on the ground. No one stopped there because it did not look useful.

One morning, Ava’s teacher told the class that they would start a project to improve a place in the neighborhood. Ava raised her hand and talked about the empty lot. She said it could become a small garden where people could enjoy flowers and vegetables.

On Saturday, Ava’s classmates came with gloves, small shovels, and bags for trash. First, they cleaned the lot. Then they planted seeds and watered the soil. Some students made signs reminding people to take care of the garden.

Weeks later, green plants filled the lot. Families stopped to look at the flowers. Later in the season, the class shared tomatoes and lettuce with neighbors. Ava smiled because the empty lot had become a place that helped many people.

Passage 2

The Book Cart

Ben loved to read, but he noticed that some children in his apartment building did not have many books at home. Some children wanted to read more, but they did not know where to find books they could keep for a few days.

Ben talked to his family about the problem. His sister suggested making a small book cart for the building lobby. Ben asked classmates to donate books they had already read. Soon, he had picture books, chapter books, and books about animals and space.

Ben placed the books on a cart with a sign that said, “Take a book. Return it when you are done.” Each week, he checked the cart and added new books. Sometimes he helped younger children choose a book they might enjoy.

After a month, more children in the building were reading. Some children even wrote short notes about their favorite books. Ben felt proud because a simple idea helped others learn and enjoy stories.

Writing Prompt

Write an essay explaining how Ava and Ben helped their communities.
